Embracing Diversity: Disability and Inclusion – Part 1

Sixteen years ago, on the 24th of July 2006, my life took an unexpected turn. I contracted Steven Johnson Syndrome due to medical negligence—a crisis that not only stole my eyesight but also dashed my childhood dreams. The loss was profound, and for a while, it felt as though my future had been irreparably altered.

Yet, when I reflect on those early, painful days, I also recognize a powerful truth: I was never alone. Surrounded by a community of unwavering support—family, friends, mentors—I learned to see beyond the immediate darkness. Their constant encouragement pushed me to take that crucial first step, to learn from the past instead of being paralyzed by it.

I often wonder how much smoother this journey might have been if the support and awareness available today had been present back then. Despite the many obstacles, the very challenges that once seemed insurmountable have become the building blocks of my resilience. They forced me to expand my thinking, to analyze life from new perspectives, and ultimately to find mentors who guided me through the storm.

Starting next week, I will be launching a series of weekly LinkedIn posts where I delve into the everyday challenges faced by people with disabilities and diversity. In these posts, I’ll share insights, personal stories, and actionable strategies for overcoming the barriers that still exist in our society. My hope is that my journey and the lessons I've learned will not only raise awareness but also inspire others to create a more inclusive world.

I am honored to have this platform to share my experiences. If my words can help even one person facing similar challenges, then every struggle I endured will have been worthwhile. Join me as we explore the power of resilience, the importance of support, and the transformative impact of seeing ability where others only see limitations.
